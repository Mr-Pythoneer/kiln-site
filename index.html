01 — The shift

One giant brain for everyone was always a phase.

For a couple of years we have all rented the same enormous, general model through the same metered API. It is remarkable — and it is a rental. Every request is billed, every prompt is sent off-site, and the model doing your most sensitive work belongs to someone else. That is not the finish line. It is the mainframe era, right before computing got personal.

02 — Custom

A model built for one job beats a giant one juggling everything.

You don't need a model that can write a sonnet, debug Rust, and plan a wedding. You need one that does your task — your format, your rules, your edge cases — correctly, every time. Narrow isn't a limitation. On the thing you actually do all day, it is the whole advantage.

03 — Personal

Trained on your data. Fluent in your world.

A general model has read the internet. Yours has learned you — your documents, your domain, your house style, the way your team actually works. It doesn't need a three-paragraph prompt to behave, because the behaviour is baked into the model itself. It shows up already knowing the job.

04 — Local & private

It runs where your data already lives.

No API, no meter, no round-trip to someone else's servers. Your model runs on your hardware — offline, if you want — and the data you were nervous about handing to a vendor never leaves the building. Privacy stops being a policy you hope holds, and becomes a fact of where the computer is.

05 — Owned

You keep it. Forever.

The model is a file you own — copy it, deploy it, run it in ten years. No subscription, no surprise deprecation, no vendor quietly changing its behaviour overnight. It isn't a tool you're licensed to touch. It's an asset that sits on your side of the table.

The future isn't one model everyone rents. It's a million private ones — each built for the person who owns it.
