The thesis

Renting a giant brain is a temporary arrangement.

Frontier models are extraordinary — and they are also a metered pipe your data flows through, priced per token, rate-limited, and changed without your consent. For most real work you don't need a model that can do everything. You need one that does your thing, perfectly, privately, forever.

01

Your data never leaves the building

Trained and run on your own GPU. No third-party API, no telemetry, no copies of your data sitting on someone else's servers. Private by construction, not by policy.

02

Specialists beat generalists — at one job

A small model tuned to a single task can match or beat a giant general model on that task, at a fraction of the cost and latency. Narrow is a feature, not a limitation.

03

You own the weights

The output is a file you keep. Run it offline on Linux, macOS, or Windows for as long as you like. No per-token bill, no rug-pull, no rate limit, no vendor lock.

04

One GPU is enough

Built to fine-tune capable models on a single consumer graphics card — and honest, up front, about what will and won't fit before you spend hours training it.
