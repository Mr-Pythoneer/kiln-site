Abstract

p.1
Pre-beta · first cohort forming

A small model that is yours beats a giant one you only rent.

Kiln trains a private, specialist language model on your examples, then hands it back. You own it outright and run it on your own hardware. No API meter, no data leaving your control.

Most teams reach for one enormous general model behind someone else's API. It is impressive, and it is a rental: every request is metered, every prompt travels off-site, and the thing doing your most sensitive work answers to a vendor, not to you.

For a narrow, repeated job — the same class of document, the same extraction, the same translation into your internal format — you do not need general genius. You need a model that has seen your task ten thousand times and nothing else. That model is small enough to own, and small enough to run where the data already lives.

§01

Thesis

p.2

Renting one giant model is the right default when you don't yet know what you need. It stops being the right default the moment a task becomes narrow, repeated, and sensitive — which is exactly the shape of most real production work. At that point the general model is overqualified, over-priced, and over-exposed to your data.

One rented general model

Broad. Metered. Off-site.
  • Every call is billed; cost grows with success
  • Your prompts and outputs leave your perimeter
  • Behaviour can change under you without notice
  • Carries capability you pay for and never use
  • Needs the network to answer at all

Many small owned specialists

Narrow. Fixed. In-house.
  • Built once — no per-token bill to use it
  • Data and inference stay inside your walls
  • Frozen weights: it behaves the same next year
  • Sized to the job, so it runs on modest hardware
  • Answers with the network unplugged

The claim is not that small models are smarter. It is that for a fixed task, an owned specialist is cheaper, private, and permanent — and you can keep a shelf of them.

§02

Topology

p.3

The rented topology routes every task, from every team, out through one shared meter. The owned topology keeps each task on a dedicated small model that lives next to the work.

§03

Procedure

p.4

Three moves, high level. You supply the task and choose the starting point; we do the specialising; you take delivery of a model that is yours to run.

STEP 01 / INPUT

Bring your examples

Show the model the job by example — inputs paired with the outputs you'd sign off on. Hundreds to thousands of real cases teach it your task and your standards.

STEP 02 / BASE

Choose a base

Pick the open model to specialise, sized to where it will run — from something that fits a laptop to something for a single server. Unsure? We advise.

STEP 03 / DELIVERY

Receive a model you own

We hand back the fine-tuned weights. You install it where the data lives and run it offline. It is a file you keep — no dependency on us to use it.

§04

Ownership & privacy

p.5

"Private" and "owned" are the whole point, so they are not features we can revoke. Ownership means you hold the weights: the model does not phone home, does not expire, and does not change behaviour because a vendor shipped an update.

Privacy is structural, not a policy. When the model runs on your hardware, your inputs never leave the building to be answered. The data you were nervous about handing to an API is the data that trains a model you keep to yourself.

This is the difference between a tool you're licensed to touch and an asset that sits on your balance sheet.

§05

Applicability

p.6

Owning a specialist pays off wherever the task is fixed and the stakes on data, cost, or latency are real. In rough order of who feels it first:

SettingWhy an owned specialist fits
Regulated industriesHealth, finance, legal — where sending records to an outside API is a compliance problem, not a convenience. Keep the model, keep the data in-house.
On-premise / air-gappedEnvironments with no outbound internet by design. A model that runs offline is the only kind that runs here at all.
Cost at scaleMillions of similar calls a month. A one-time build replaces a per-token invoice that grows every time the product succeeds.
Edge & on-deviceField hardware, kiosks, vehicles, phones — where a round-trip to a datacentre is too slow, too flaky, or simply unavailable.
Domain specialistsA house dialect, an internal DSL, a narrow classification your team does all day. A model tuned to only that beats a generalist guessing at it.
